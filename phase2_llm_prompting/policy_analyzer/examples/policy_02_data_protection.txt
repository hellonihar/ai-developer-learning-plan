DATA PROTECTION AND PRIVACY POLICY

Policy Number: DPP-2024-003
Effective Date: March 15, 2024
Applicable To: All employees, contractors, and third-party data processors of TechNova Inc.

---

1. SCOPE AND PURPOSE

This Policy establishes the requirements for the collection, processing, storage, transfer, and disposal of personal data in compliance with GDPR (EU Regulation 2016/679), CCPA (California Civil Code Section 1798.100), and PIPEDA (Canada).

---

2. DATA CLASSIFICATION

All data shall be classified into one of three tiers:

Tier 1 - Public Data: Information intended for public dissemination. No access controls required.

Tier 2 - Internal Data: Confidential business information not approved for public release. Requires role-based access control (RBAC).

Tier 3 - Restricted Data: Personal identifiable information (PII), protected health information (PHI), financial records, or any data subject to regulatory protection. Requires encryption at rest (AES-256) and in transit (TLS 1.2+), multi-factor authentication, and full audit logging.

---

3. DATA SUBJECT RIGHTS

3.1 Right of Access
Data subjects may request confirmation of whether their personal data is being processed and access to their data. Requests must be fulfilled within 30 calendar days.

3.2 Right to Erasure
Data subjects may request deletion of their personal data. The Company shall comply within 30 calendar days unless retention is required by applicable law or ongoing legal proceedings.

3.3 Right to Data Portability
Data subjects may request transfer of their data to another controller in a structured, commonly used, machine-readable format.

3.4 Right to Object
Data subjects may object to processing of their data for direct marketing purposes. Such objections shall be honored immediately and without exception.

---

4. DATA BREACH PROCEDURES

4.1 Breach Notification
Any employee who discovers or suspects a data breach must report the incident to the Data Protection Officer (DPO) within 2 hours.

4.2 External Notification
The DPO shall notify the relevant supervisory authority within 72 hours of becoming aware of a breach that is likely to result in a risk to the rights and freedoms of natural persons.

4.3 Documentation
All breaches, including near-misses, shall be documented in the Breach Register maintained by the DPO. Documentation must include the root cause, impact assessment, remediation steps, and lessons learned.

---

5. THIRD-PARTY PROCESSORS

5.1 Due Diligence
Prior to engaging any third-party data processor, the Company shall conduct a data protection impact assessment (DPIA) and verify the processor's compliance with ISO 27001 or equivalent certification.

5.2 Data Processing Agreements
All third-party processors must execute a Data Processing Agreement (DPA) that includes:
a) Clear instructions for data processing;
b) Confidentiality obligations;
c) Security measures at least equivalent to those in Section 2;
d) Assistance with data subject requests;
e) Notification obligations in case of breach;
f) Right to audit;
g) Deletion or return of data upon termination.

5.3 Sub-processing
Third-party processors may not engage sub-processors without prior written authorization from the Company. Any authorized sub-processor shall be subject to equivalent contractual obligations.

---

6. DATA RETENTION AND DISPOSAL

6.1 Retention Periods
Personal data shall not be retained longer than necessary. Standard retention periods are:
- Employee records: 7 years after termination
- Customer records: 5 years after last interaction
- Marketing data: 2 years
- Website analytics: 26 months

6.2 Secure Disposal
Data beyond its retention period must be securely disposed of using methods appropriate to the data tier: degaussing or physical destruction for physical media; cryptographic erasure or secure overwrite (DoD 5220.22-M standard) for digital data.

---

7. ENFORCEMENT AND PENALTIES

Violation of this Policy may result in disciplinary action up to and including termination of employment and legal action. Violations by third-party processors may result in contract termination and reporting to the relevant supervisory authority.

---

8. REVIEW AND AMENDMENT

This Policy shall be reviewed annually by the Data Protection Officer and the Chief Information Security Officer. Amendments require approval from the Board of Directors.

---

| Algorithm | Key Length | Use Case |
|-----------|-----------|----------|

| RSA | 2048-bit | Key exchange |
| ECDSA | P-256 | Digital signatures |
