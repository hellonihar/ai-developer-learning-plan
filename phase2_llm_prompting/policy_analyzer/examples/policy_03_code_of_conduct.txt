EMPLOYEE CODE OF CONDUCT AND CORPORATE GOVERNANCE POLICY

Policy Number: HR-2024-007
Effective Date: January 1, 2024
Applicable To: All employees, officers, directors, and contractors of GlobalVentures Corp.

---

1. CORE PRINCIPLES

All personnel shall act with integrity, honesty, and in compliance with applicable laws and regulations. This Code applies to all business activities and locations worldwide.

---

2. CONFLICTS OF INTEREST

2.1 Prohibition
Employees shall not engage in any activity, investment, or relationship that conflicts with the interests of the Company.

2.2 Disclosure
Any actual or potential conflict of interest must be disclosed in writing to the Compliance Officer within 14 days of becoming aware of the conflict.

2.3 Gifts and Entertainment
Employees may not accept gifts, entertainment, or hospitality from any business partner with a value exceeding $100 USD annually per source. All gifts must be reported to the Compliance Officer.

---

3. CONFIDENTIALITY AND INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY

3.1 Confidential Information
Employees shall not disclose, distribute, or use confidential information except as necessary to perform their duties. Confidential information includes but is not limited to trade secrets, financial data, customer lists, business strategies, and proprietary technology.

3.2 Assignment of IP
Any invention, work product, or intellectual property created by an employee during the course of employment shall be the sole and exclusive property of the Company. This obligation survives termination of employment.

3.3 Non-Compete
For a period of 12 months following termination of employment for any reason, employees shall not engage in any business that competes with the Company within a 50-mile radius of any Company office.

---

4. INSIDER TRADING

4.1 Prohibition
Employees in possession of material, non-public information regarding the Company or any business partner shall not trade in securities of such entities.

4.2 Blackout Periods
All employees with access to quarterly financial results are prohibited from trading Company securities during the period beginning 30 days before the end of each fiscal quarter and ending 48 hours after public release of results.

---

5. ANTI-BRIBERY AND CORRUPTION

5.1 Prohibition
No employee shall offer, promise, give, authorize, or receive any bribe, kickback, or improper payment, directly or indirectly, to or from any person or entity, including government officials, for the purpose of obtaining or retaining business.

5.2 Facilitation Payments
Facilitation payments (small payments to expedite routine government actions) are strictly prohibited regardless of local custom or practice.

5.3 Third-Party Due Diligence
Prior to engaging any third-party representative, agent, or consultant who will interact with government officials, enhanced due diligence must be completed. This includes background checks, anti-corruption training, and written contractual compliance undertakings.

---

6. REPORTING VIOLATIONS (WHISTLEBLOWER POLICY)

6.1 Reporting Channels
Employees may report suspected violations anonymously through the Company's Ethics Hotline (1-800-555-0199) or via the Compliance Portal at compliance.globalventures.com.

6.2 Non-Retaliation
The Company strictly prohibits retaliation against any employee who reports a suspected violation in good faith. Any employee found to have engaged in retaliation shall be subject to disciplinary action, up to and including termination.

6.3 False Reports
Knowingly making a false report is a violation of this Code and may result in disciplinary action.

---

7. SOCIAL MEDIA AND PUBLIC COMMUNICATIONS

7.1 Personal Social Media
Employees must clearly identify that their personal social media posts represent their own views, not those of the Company. Employees shall not disclose confidential information or make disparaging remarks about the Company, its officers, employees, customers, or partners.

7.2 Media Inquiries
All inquiries from the media or investment community must be directed to the Corporate Communications Department. Employees are not authorized to speak on behalf of the Company without prior approval.

---

8. COMPLIANCE AND ENFORCEMENT

8.1 Training
All employees must complete Code of Conduct training annually. Failure to complete training may result in suspension of travel and expense reimbursement privileges.

8.2 Investigations
The Company reserves the right to investigate any suspected violation, including reviewing company-issued devices, email communications, and access logs.

8.3 Penalties
Violations of this Code may result in disciplinary action, including written warning, suspension, forfeiture of compensation, termination of employment, and referral for criminal prosecution.

---

9. ACKNOWLEDGMENT

All employees must sign an acknowledgment confirming they have read, understood, and agree to comply with this Code. Failure to sign the acknowledgment does not relieve an employee from compliance obligations.
